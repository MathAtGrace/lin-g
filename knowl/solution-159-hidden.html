1. The vectors \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_2\) that form the columns of \(A\) are found by rotating the standard basis vectors \(\evec_1\) and \(\evec_2\text{.}\) Consequently, they are positively oriented and form a \(1\times1\) square. This says that \(\det(A) = 1\text{.}\)

  2. The vectors \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_2\) that form the columns of \(B\) are found by reflecting the standard basis vectors \(\evec_1\) and \(\evec_2\text{.}\) Consequently, they are negatively oriented and form a \(1\times1\) square. This says that \(\det(B) = -1\text{.}\)
